CERSAIE '12

Spain is innovation. It is not secret that Spain has a long history in the field of ceramics, pioneering new techniques for centuries. Although today’s industry has its origin in this noble handcrafted tradition, the process has evolved over the last 30 years into a leading high-tech business with great success in today’s world markets.

The Spanish presence at CERSAIE is the major foreign contingent at the exhibition. This year the Spanish pavilion will showcase 93 exhibitors in more than 7,800 square meters.

See, touch, feel and experience our ceramic tiles in any of our exhibitors’ spaces. Most new innovations have emerged at the hands of Spanish companies: digital inkjet printing systems, rectified and slim tiles, ceramic ventilated façades, etc. This will continue and most of the new innovations to come will emerge once more from Spain.

Tile of Spain, a quality product that brings you new trends, applications, architectural and sustainable uses to create spaces with more human dimension. We hope that you will visit us and that you will enjoy a brief taste of what Spain has to offer.

Tile of Spain

As the voice of the industry, ASCER (the Ceramic Tile Manufacturers’ Association) promotes ceramic tiles through the collective brand “Tile of Spain”. This is a joint effort of the members of ASCER, with the support of the Spanish Institute of Foreign Trade (ICEX).

In 2011, the Spanish tile manufacturing industry’s turnover was 2,597 million euros, 70% of which were earned in exports to more than 180 different countries. Today, Spain is the second biggest exporter of ceramic tiles in Europe, and the third in the world. The industry is the third sector that brings more trade surplus to Spain, and the first in commercial coverage.

To learn more about Tile of Spain, its companies and innovative products, you can subscribe -for free- to the magazine Ceraspaña at www.ceraspana.es

ASCER, the ceramic tile manufacturers’ association in Spain, represents almost 150 member companies, that is, 95% of the national production. Their purpose is to support, represent and promote the general and common interests of the ceramic tile sector, providing a wide set of services to its members, as well as combined activities which enable the companies to obtain superior results.
